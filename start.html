The Martingale Betting System is one of the best known mathematical fallacies related to gambling. The article on Wikipedia clarifies things from a mathematical point of view, but basically the fallacy states that by doubling the bet, when the odds are 50%, or 1 in 2 (such as two colors, a coin toss...) the player must eventually win.

Without doing any analysis, many people believe that this means that when betting larger sums, an eventual win will mean an earning of the ammount that was gambled. Of course, this ignores, on the first instance, the cost of previous bets. It is mathematically proven that for each Martingale game, only the initial quantity that was bet will be won. For instance, if we started with 1 €, 2 €, 4 €, 8 € and then won a 16 € bet, it would mean that we had to spend 1+2+4+8 = 15 on it, thus winning only 1 €.

If we wanted a quicker win, let's assume an initial bet of 50 €. The quantity would quickly increase: 50 €, 100 €, 200 €, 400 €, 800 €, 1.600 €... with unlimited money and time, we will eventually win. As most casinos impose credit limits, this method will quickly result in losing everything. The Gambler's Fallacy is the unfounded belief that these outcomes have memory. That if we keep betting on black, once many reds appear, black must eventually appear. The truth is that the oods are always 1/2.
